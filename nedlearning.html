NED Learning

Challenge

During Startup Weekend, I meet a group looking to bring the Adaptive Learning Model to mainstream education. They wanted to bring it to classrooms in an easy to follow format with an eventual ED-Tech component.

My Roles

Product Strategy, Creative Director, UX Designer, Graphic Designer

Solution

Step 1: Initial discovery

To understand the program we meet with the founder, T. Kieran Oâ€™Mahony who had been teaching this model for about five years. Stephen Morrissey and I reviewed the process, results, and future plans that Kieran had set.

Step 2: Branding

I facilitated a branding meetings with the team and we agreed to Neural EDaptive as a working descriptive title referencing Neural, Education, and Adaptation. This branding changed when the NED the character was born and became NED Learning.

Step 3: Tech Decision

Kieran and his backers had originally planned to develop a SaaS offering. As I looked at the plan I felt there was an important testing step missing. A simple paper version would prove our concept and pave the way to a more tech solution.

Step 4: NED Challenge Board

Taking Kieran's process for learning, we developed the Challenge Board. The Challenge Board was both a prototype for development and a physical product for those classes that were unable to have tech in their classrooms. In a short time we had mapped out Kieran's model in the board and created an easy to follow process for the students and teachers

Step 5: NED Character

We tried a few different designs for NED and settled on leaving facial features off and focused on expressing moods and feelings through body language instead. So far this has been well received and having the viewer project their own feelings on NED makes him much more versatile.

Step 6: Prototype 1

We developed the first prototype and we are now testing it in schools. Here is the digital version.

Step 7: NED Notes

We also developed a stylized worksheet form to help guide students and give them an engaging way to record the information they need to gather during the process. It pulls from the same styles as the board to make it a cohesive and easy to follow guide.

Step 8: Testing

WE have tested it in a few schools and have teachers signing up to participate. We intend to get a lot of qualitative observational data to help with prototyping and start a quantitative baseline for some performance metrics down the road.
