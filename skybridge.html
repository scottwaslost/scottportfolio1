Skybridge Solutions

Challenge

Skybridge came to us with an interesting challenge, they needed branding and a marketing site aimed at attracting small and medium size businesses in the tech sector who are looking for a manufacturing partner.

My Roles

UX Designer, Brand Lead, Graphic Designer, and a Researcher.

Solution

Step 1: Initial discovery

We started with owner interviews to understand the business, culture, and goals that the company was looking to achieve. This gave us an idea of the vision, passion, and dreams behind their business and we started to understand the company’s identity.

Step 2: Competitive Research

Looking across the market and who was supplying who, how others were represented themselves, how they were finding success. Getting the clients' impressions on these was helpful in identifying their preferences right away.

Step 3: Brand and Name Research

Next we looked at who was using the name Skybridge and what logo and styling designs to avoid. We also validated the space for using the name Skybridge in that vertical.

Step 4: Potential Clients Research

Then we looked at their ideal clients from the stakeholder interview information about target company size, traction, and type of products. These brands and logos gave us an idea of what branding may have the most impact, and which styles to avoid. We came up with some great examples representing the most upcoming companies that could need Skybridge’s services while comparing them to their current clients.

Step 5: Slogan and Message Research

During all this research we kept track of the taglines and stories that other brands were using. This exploration helped us understand what messages were key for Skybridge. This part of the research was especially important for understanding what the website should display.

Step 6: Font Discovery

Next we dove into the vast sea of fonts that exist to find something unique that resonated with the message we wanted. Modern, edgy, and simple was often our clients’ motto and we definitely agreed for the customers they were targeting.

Step 7: Color Exploration

At this point we started considering what colors would distinguish Skybridge. We gravitated towards blue due to the Skybridge name and blue’s psychological association with trust and knowledge.

Step 8: Logo Creation

In creating a logo we did a series of different designs in order to find a path that spoke to the company’s identity. Use of all the previous research become apparent here as we explored designs that empowered the brand. Using psychology about shapes, angles, and color the first drafts were born.

Step 9: Logo Refinement

Following the initial creation, we presented and received feedback from the clients over the course of meetings both in person and remote. These meetings were key to the finishing process of the logo as many opinions played a part in the final decision. As you can see through the refinement process color choices emerged and stylizing became more refined.

In the end, an amazing logo was chosen that stands out in their marketplace. It embodies the messages the client wanted, a history of trust, an easy solution, and a longterm relationship. We felt this was accomplished by using the imagery of a mountain to symbolize history, a transparent stripe to pull it together, a beacon to signify easy solutions, and bridge symbolism to signify a lasting relationship. Together these elements both resonated with the clients and speaks to the NW tech culture that they wanted to attract.

Step 10: UX Discovery

Our strategy was to develop a simple marketing site that quickly informs the user of the value and offerings available with a straightforward call to action. Was chose a few different templates that had high potential and the client helped pick the most appropriate. The blocks were then arranged to best display the company’s value while grabbing the viewer's interest.

Industry and User Research

In this step we finished the industry research and needed the user’s perspective. Using surveys and previously published articles we discovered some of the prefered layouts for website engagement looking closely at how the users would be engaged in story, process, past work, team, and contact.

Creating User Personas

For this part we created 2 user personas wrapped around the potential client research that we had obtained.

Ideation

Next we did an ideation session looking at the different ways that these personas would interact and identify with the content in the website, the flow, and design.

Scenarios

After sketching out a couple scenarios we checked them against different layouts and calls to action within the website.

Prototyping

We did an AB test with a few users that fit our personas having the website laid out in different flows.

Final Product

At the end of the project we had a unique brand with an engaging website. Our clients were extremely happy and continue to get great customer feedback and responses.
